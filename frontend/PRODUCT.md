# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Government immigration agents (AGENTE role) and system administrators (ADMIN role) employed by Panama's Servicio Nacional de Migración (SNM). They work at desktop workstations in a professional office environment, processing immigration applications under legal and compliance mandates. Secondary surface: foreign citizens who submit their own application and check its status.

## Product Purpose

SIDEM-PAN is an intelligent immigration due-diligence platform for Panama. It automates risk scoring (INTERPOL, OFAC SDN screening, country restrictions), routes cases to agents for dictamen, handles document subsanación flows, and maintains a WORM audit trail. Governed by Decreto Ley 3 de 2008. Success means agents process cases faster with fewer compliance errors and a defensible audit record.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative · Precise · Trustworthy. The system represents the Panamanian state; its interface must convey institutional weight and reliability, not startup energy. Tone: calm, formal, declarative. No puns, no emoji, no marketing speak.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (colorful KPI tiles, gradients everywhere, rounded everything)
- Consumer fintech apps (playful, bright, casual)
- Anything that looks like Notion, Linear, or a landing-page builder output

## Design Principles

1. **Authority through restraint** — institutional navy + gold is the only palette; suppress the impulse to add accent colors, illustrations, or decoration that dilutes gravitas.
2. **Density earns its keep** — data tables and detail panels must pack information legibly; whitespace serves hierarchy, not aesthetics.
3. **State clarity above all** — every status (PENDIENTE, EN_EVALUACION, SUBSANACION_PENDIENTE, APROBADO, RECHAZADO) must be immediately distinguishable at a glance.
4. **Typography carries authority** — Libre Baskerville for headings signals institutional permanence; IBM Plex Sans for body signals precision; never mix decorative or playful faces.
5. **Progressive disclosure** — the queue table is the workhorse; detail and dictamen panels are reached by intent, not by accident.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. All text must meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio. Keyboard navigation required throughout. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color must never be the sole indicator of state (badges must carry text labels). Spanish language only.
